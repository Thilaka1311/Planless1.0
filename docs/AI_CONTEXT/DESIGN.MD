# Planless Design System v1

## Product Vision

Planless is a spontaneous coordination platform that helps people quickly organize real-world activities with friends and circles.

The product should feel alive, social, premium, and active.

Users should feel:

* Something is happening right now
* Joining is easy
* Friends are active
* Plans are real
* The app is trustworthy

---

# Design Personality

## Primary Traits

* Premium

## Avoid

* Corporate
* Dashboard-heavy
* Gaming UI
* Neon cyberpunk
* Cartoonish
* Dating-app aesthetics
* Overly playful

# Core Design Principles

## 1. People Create Energy

Always prioritize:

* Participants
* Hosts
* Circles
* Avatars

Never design around empty metrics.

---

## 2. Plans Are The Product

The Plan Card is the most important component in the app.

The Plan Card receives the highest design attention.

---

## 3. Photography Over Icons

Prefer:

* Real-world photography
* Activity imagery
* People

Avoid:

* Emoji-heavy interfaces
* Generic icons as primary visuals

---

## 4. Hierarchy Before Decoration

Premium design comes from:

* Layout
* Typography
* Spacing

Not:

* Borders
* Effects
* Gradients

---

## 5. Dark Mode Philosophy

Dark surfaces should feel rich and premium.

Use contrast and spacing.

Avoid excessive outlines.

---

# Component Rules

## Plan Cards

Use:

* Large imagery
* Strong title
* Time
* Location
* Participants
* CTA

Plan cards should feel like invitations.

---

## Circle Cards

Show:

* Circle Name
* Member Avatars
* Active Plans

Do not focus on statistics alone.

---

## Create Flow

The Create screen should feel like starting an experience.

Not filling out a form.

Activity cards should use immersive imagery.

---

## Empty States

Never feel dead.

Always encourage action.

Examples:

Explore Circles

Create A Plan

Invite Friends

Discover Activities

---

# Premium + Energetic Test

Before approving any screen ask:

1. Does it feel premium?
2. Does it feel alive?
3. Does it feel social?
4. Does it encourage action?
5. Does it match the Home screen?

If the answer to any is no, redesign.
