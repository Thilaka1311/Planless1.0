# PLANLESS MVP PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY, BEHAVIORAL & UI DESIGN RULES

This document serves as the official and permanent product philosophy, interaction style guide, UI design laws, and design reference system for Planless.

Every future screen, component, flow, interaction, database decision, and feature generated for Planless must adhere consistently to the constraints, values, and guidelines defined herein.

---

# PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY RULES

## 1. Social Coordination Focus

Planless is a social coordination platform built strictly to help real-world cohorts discover, align, and execute real-world plans quickly and effortlessly.

---

## 2. The Plan is the Core Object

The primary building block of Planless is the Plan.

The core focus is not user posts, creator feeds, public influencer scores, or algorithmic content consumption.

The core focus is facilitating offline connection and shared experiences.

---

## 3. The Core Loop

Every major interaction in the application revolves around:

Discovery

→ Plan

→ Experience

→ Memory

The discovery moment is a core part of the product.

Without discovery there is no plan.

Without plans there are no shared experiences.

Without experiences there are no memories.

---

## 4. Friction Elimination

The application exists to reduce the administrative friction between:

"I want to do something."

and

"The plan is happening."

Every interaction should move users closer to real-world participation with as little effort as possible.

---

## 5. Real-World Priority

The product prioritizes genuine real-world interactions over prolonged in-app engagement.

Success is measured by plans happening in real life, not by screen time.

---

## 6. Emotional Tone & Aesthetic Vibe

The look, feel, and wording of the app should be:

* Simple
* Social-first
* Lightweight
* Calm
* Modern
* Human
* Culturally real

Avoid:

* corporate language
* enterprise terminology
* robotic system messaging

---

## 7. What Planless Is NOT

Planless is strictly NOT:

* Discord
* Meetup
* Eventbrite
* Splitwise
* A productivity tool
* A project management tool
* A Kanban board
* A traditional content platform
* A dating application
* A professional networking platform
* An influencer platform
* A community forum
* An interest-based social network

The product exists for real-world coordination.

---

## 8. Versatility of Plans

Plans may be:

### Spontaneous

Example:

Football tonight at 8 PM.

### Recurring

Example:

Weekly Thursday movie night.

### Scheduled

Example:

Weekend road trip.

### Culturally Anticipated

Example:

Marvel premiere watch party.

All four categories are equally important.

---

## 9. The 30-Second Mandate

A user must be able to:

* Create a plan
* Configure a plan
* Publish a plan
* Join a plan
* Skip a plan

in under 30 seconds.

The product should aggressively minimize unnecessary input.

---

## 10. MVP Simplicity Over Feature Depth

Clean flows are more valuable than feature-rich complexity.

Every new feature should justify its existence.

When in doubt:

Choose simplicity.

---

## 11. Cognitive Load Reduction

Every screen should aggressively remove unnecessary complexity.

Avoid:

* excessive filters
* crowded layouts
* analytics-heavy views
* large configuration surfaces

The user should rarely need to think about the system itself.

---

## 12. Design Inspiration

### Instagram

For:

* profile simplicity
* clean identity presentation

### Spotify

For:

* calm dark surfaces
* premium feeling
* visual hierarchy

### Opal

For:

* generous spacing
* rounded layouts
* visual breathing room

These are references, not templates.

---

## 13. Participation Should Feel Exciting

Joining a plan should feel like accepting an invitation.

Use:

* attendee face piles
* lightweight social proof
* tactile interactions
* confident animations

Avoid:

* administrative receipts
* transactional confirmations
* enterprise workflows

---

## 14. Metric Zero

Avoid:

* follower counts
* following counts
* public rankings
* engagement metrics
* creator leaderboards
* popularity scores

Planless is not a social-status platform.

---

## 15. Coordination Above Consumption

Every screen should encourage:

* planning
* joining
* coordinating
* attending
* remembering

Content consumption is secondary.

---

## 16. Desktop-First Execution, Mobile-First Constraints

Planless should function well at all sizes.

However:

* touch ergonomics
* thumb reachability
* swipe gestures
* mobile-first interactions

should always be prioritized.

---

## 17. Speed Beats Complexity

Fast feedback loops are preferable to complex systems.

Examples:

* instant joins
* instant skips
* instant waitlisting
* immediate visual feedback

Users should feel momentum.

---

## 18. Warmth of Design

Use:

* dark slate surfaces
* rounded corners
* soft shadows
* calm contrast
* warm accent colors

The interface should feel premium but approachable.

---

## 19. Real-Life Snapshot Concept

Planless should feel like:

"Instagram for real-world experiences."

The product is not about documenting life.

It is about creating opportunities for life to happen.

---

## 20. Circles Are Coordination Units

Circles are trusted social coordination units.

A Circle is not:

* a community
* an audience
* a forum
* a content group

A Circle exists to make planning easier among people who already know each other.

Circle membership determines future plan visibility.

---

## 21. Discovery Is Effortless

Users should never need to search extensively for things to do.

The Home Feed should continuously surface relevant real-world opportunities one plan at a time.

The feed is a discovery surface, not a content feed.

---

## 22. Participation Should Feel Reversible

Users should feel comfortable:

* joining
* skipping
* leaving
* rejoining
* waitlisting

without social friction.

Planless should encourage participation rather than punish indecision.

---

## 23. Capacity Should Never Create Dead Ends

When plans are full:

Users should still be able to express intent to participate.

Waitlists should feel:

* lightweight
* automatic
* fair
* frictionless

Host approval should not be required for standard waitlist promotion.

---

## 24. Database-Driven Product

Planless is a database-driven application.

The database is the source of truth for:

* participation state
* waitlist state
* plan visibility
* host ownership
* circle membership

UI state must never be considered authoritative.

Every user action must persist successfully before becoming canonical.

---

## 25. Discovery First, Administration Second

Users should spend most of their time:

* discovering plans
* joining plans
* coordinating with friends

They should spend very little time:

* configuring settings
* managing systems
* administering workflows

Administration should always remain secondary to participation.

---

# FINAL PRINCIPLE

Every decision in Planless should answer one question:

"Does this make it easier for people to discover, coordinate, and experience real-world plans together?"

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.
